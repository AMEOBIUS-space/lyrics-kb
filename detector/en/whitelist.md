# EN Detector — whitelist (false-positive guards)

Parallel spirit to RU white-list 25.27: some patterns are **genre law**, not slop.

---

## W1 · Mantra / accumulation chorus

**Looks like:** E06 chorus_checklist or E10 uniform length  
**Allow when:** one slot in the repeated frame **changes** and meaning accumulates.

```
water remembers
brick remembers
you will too
```

Not allowed: *I'll love / I'll fight / I'll stay* (slots interchangeable).

---

## W2 · Intentional cliché (irony frame)

**Looks like:** E08 cliche_rhyme_pair  
**Allow when:** surrounding lines clearly ironize or break the pair (*"love/above — yeah, I know how that song goes"*).

Document the irony in the audit note. If irony is only in the writer's head — flag stands.

---

## W3 · Sparse identical stress (chant genres)

**Looks like:** E10 uniform_line_length  
**Allow when:** section is explicitly chant/post-chorus/gospel call and length is a groove weapon.

---

## W4 · Second-person hypophora as hook device

**Looks like:** E04 hypophora  
**Allow when:** question is the hook and **never answered** in-lyric (open wound), or answered only by an image two sections later.

---

## W5 · Genre stock used once as door

**Looks like:** E16 genre_autopilot  
**Allow when:** exactly one stock image opens the scene, then local detail takes over for the rest of the section.

---

## W6 · Abstract title paid in concrete body

**Looks like:** E11 abstract_stack in chorus  
**Allow when:** chorus claim is abstract but **every verse line** converts it to sense-bound evidence.

---

## Audit note format

```
WHITELIST: W1 on lines 12–15 — slot "remembers" object changes (water→brick→you)
```
