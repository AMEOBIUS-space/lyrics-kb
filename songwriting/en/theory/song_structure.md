# Song structure — sections, contrast, and the "where are we" map

Structure is navigation. The listener should always know *where they are* in the journey — and feel why this section exists now.

---

## 1. Core sections

| Section | Job | Lyric density | Typical length |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Intro** | world-entry; motif | none / hook fragment | 2–8 bars |
| **Verse** | story, detail, setup | high | 8–16 bars |
| **Pre-chorus** | lift, instability | medium, rising | 4–8 bars |
| **Chorus** | thesis, title, payoff | low words / high punch | 8–16 bars |
| **Post-chorus** | earworm tag after chorus | minimal | 2–8 bars |
| **Bridge** | new angle, break pattern | medium; contrast | 8 bars |
| **Breakdown** | strip arrangement | sparse | 4–8 bars |
| **Outro** | exit; echo title | falling | 4–16 bars |

---

## 2. Common full forms

**Pop default:**
```
Intro → V1 → Pre → Chorus → V2 → Pre → Chorus → Bridge → Chorus → Outro
```

**Verse-heavy / folk-narrative:**
```
V1 → V2 → Chorus → V3 → Chorus → Outro
```

**AABA (standards / classic):**
```
A → A → B (bridge) → A
```

**EDM / drop form:**
```
Intro → Build → Drop → Verse/Break → Build → Drop → Outro
```

**Through-composed:** minimal repetition; art-song / progressive. Harder for memory; strong for story.

---

## 3. Section contrast (the real job of structure)

If verse and chorus feel the same, you don't have a chorus — you have two verses.

Contrast axes (move at least two when entering chorus):
- **Register** — lower speech-like verse → higher sung chorus
- **Note length** — shorter verse syllables → longer chorus vowels
- **Rhyme strength** — looser verse → tighter chorus
- **Pronoun / POV** — "I saw…" verse → "We are…" or direct "You…" chorus
- **Time** — past-tense story → present-tense claim
- **Density** — more words in verse; fewer, repeated in chorus

---

## 4. The title as center of gravity

- Place the title where memory sticks: downbeat of chorus, end of chorus line 1 or 2, or post-chorus tag.
- Title should be **singable** (open vowels) and **stable** (prosody landing).
- Avoid burying the title mid-verse only.

**Title tests:**
1. Can a stranger text it after one listen?
2. Does it mean something alone (not only inside the story)?
3. Does the rest of the lyric orbit it without repeating it mindlessly?

---

## 5. Pre-chorus craft

Job: make the chorus inevitable.

Tools:
- rising melody / rising phrase length
- unstable endings
- harmonic climb (or held tension without resolution)
- lyric that asks or leans (*"and if I call…"*) without answering

Don't put the title in the pre-chorus unless you intentionally tease it.

---

## 6. Bridge craft

Job: break the loop so the final chorus hits harder.

Tools:
- new information (twist, admission, time-jump)
- POV shift
- thinner or thicker texture (opposite of chorus)
- modulation (use sparingly; cliché if unmotivated)

Bridge that restates the chorus idea in new adjectives = failed bridge.

---

## 7. Post-chorus and hooks

Post-chorus = pure hook after the lyrical chorus:
- chant, syllable hook (*oh-oh*), title echo, melodic tag
- low semantic load, high identity

Useful when chorus carries story and you still need a TikTok-sized fragment.

---

## 8. Information architecture across the song

| Pass | What the listener should gain |
|---|---|
| V1 | who / where / what's wrong |
| Chorus 1 | emotional thesis |
| V2 | complication, new detail (not V1 paraphrase) |
| Chorus 2 | thesis with weight of V2 |
| Bridge | reframe or cost |
| Final chorus | earned repetition; optional lyric variation on last line |

**V2 rule:** at least one image or fact that could not appear in V1.

## 10. Checklist

- [ ] Each section has a distinct job?
- [ ] Chorus contrasts verse on ≥2 axes?
- [ ] Title placement is memorable and stable?
- [ ] V2 adds information?
- [ ] Bridge is not a chorus paraphrase?
- [ ] Listener can map "where we are" cold?
