# Rhyme types — sound relationships and emotional weight

Rhyme is not decoration. It is a sonic expectation machine: set up a pattern, pay it off or break it on purpose.

Lineage: Pat Pattison rhyme families; common craft vocabulary from Berklee / Nashville / pop writing rooms.

---

## 1. Perfect rhyme

Same vowel + same final consonants from the stressed vowel on:
- *light / night*, *flame / name*, *go / show*

**Weight:** strongest resolution. Overuse = nursery-rhyme or AI-default feel.
**Use:** title pairs, chorus landings, punchlines.

---

## 2. Family rhyme (Pattison)

Same vowel family + related (not identical) consonants — plosive with plosive, nasal with nasal, etc.
- *late / shape* (t/p — both stops)
- *run / love* (n/v — related sonorants in loose family use; judge by ear)
- *kick / hit*

**Weight:** almost-perfect; feels crafted, less sing-song.
**Use:** verses, when perfect options are cliché.

---

## 3. Additive / subtractive rhyme

- **Additive:** second rhyme adds a consonant — *lay / late*, *free / freed*
- **Subtractive:** second drops a consonant — *east / ease*, *cold / coal*

**Weight:** slight surprise; good for conversational tone.

---

## 4. Assonance and consonance

- **Assonance:** shared vowel, different consonants — *fade / pain*, *ghost / road*
- **Consonance:** shared consonants, different vowels — *blank / think*, *stroke / streak*

**Weight:** loose glue; holds sections without locking them. Dominant in modern indie and alt-pop.

---

## 5. Internal rhyme

Rhyme inside a line, not only at the end:
- *I pack a bag of black and blue*
- *cold gold on the windowsill*

**Weight:** density and forward motion. Rap default; sung pop in moderation.

---

## 6. Rhyme schemes and emotional shape

| Scheme | Shape | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| **AABB** | couplets | simple, driving, nursery risk |
| **ABAB** | cross | balanced, classic |
| **ABBA** | envelope | closed thought, art-pop |
| **AAAA** | mono | obsession, mantra, threat of monotony |
| **XAXA** | sparse | conversational, modern |
| **free / slant-heavy** | irregular | confessional, indie |

**Rule:** scheme should change when the section's job changes. Verse ABAB → chorus AABB is a common lift.

---

## 7. Cliché rhyme pairs (EN red flags)

Treat as guilty until reinvented:

- fire / desire / higher
- heart / apart / start
- love / above / enough *(enough is slant — still overused)*
- night / light / right / fight
- eyes / skies / lies / cry
- together / forever
- rain / pain / again
- home / alone
- true / you / blue
- world / girl

**Repair:** keep one word, replace its pair with a concrete unexpected partner (*heart / parking lot*; *rain / change jar*).

---

## 8. Identity and rich rhyme

- **Identity:** same word or mere inflection — *love / love*, *run / running* — usually weak unless mantra.
- **Rich rhyme (rime riche):** same sound, different meaning — *raise / raze*, *knight / night* — flashy; use sparingly.

---

## 9. Rhyme and meaning (prosody of sense)

A rhyme that only exists for sound is a tax on meaning. Prefer:
1. Meaning first
2. Stress alignment second
3. Rhyme type third

If you must break a perfect rhyme to keep a true image — break it.

---

## 10. Checklist

- [ ] Chorus uses stronger rhyme resolution than verse?
- [ ] Any cliché pairs without a twist?
- [ ] At least some family/assonance (not wall-to-wall perfect)?
- [ ] Internal rhyme only where density is wanted?
- [ ] Rhyme scheme shifts between sections?
