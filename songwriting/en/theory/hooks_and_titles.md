# Hooks & titles — center of gravity

The title is the song's name and often its thesis. The hook is what lodges in the body after the song ends — sometimes the title, sometimes a melodic/lyric fragment beside it.

---

## 1. Title types

| Type | Example shape | Strength |
|---|---|---|
| **Object** | "Red Dress", "Midnight Train" | concrete, filmable |
| **Claim** | "I'm Not Sorry", "We Made It" | attitude, chorus-ready |
| **Question** | "Where Do We Go?" | unstable; needs answer or refusal |
| **Image** | "Glass House", "Paper Crowns" | metaphoric; risk of vagueness |
| **Phrase-from-speech** | "You Good?", "Text Me When" | modern, intimate |
| **Name / place** | "Caroline", "Phoenix" | identity; needs detail support |

Prefer titles you can say in a bar conversation without explanation.

---

## 2. Hook types

- **Lyric hook** — repeated phrase with sense (*"don't call me up"*)
- **Melodic hook** — tune fragment that works on *la*
- **Rhythmic hook** — chant / syncopated cell
- **Sonic hook** — production motif (not lyric's job, but writers should leave space)
- **Title hook** — title = hook (cleanest commercial case)

A song can carry more than one; one should dominate.

---

## 3. Placement map

**Strong placements:**
1. First line of chorus
2. Last line of chorus
3. Both (bookend)
4. Post-chorus tag
5. Opening vocal cold (cold open title — bold)

**Weak placements:**
- Only once in verse 2
- Buried mid-line with no stress
- Changed every chorus without a reason

---

## 4. Title–lyric orbit

Every verse image should make the title more true, more bitter, or more ironic by the end.

Test: delete the title from the chorus. If the song still "has a name" in your head, the orbit is weak — the title isn't doing work.

---

## 5. Singability rules for titles

- Prefer open vowels on the money note: *ah, oh, ay, ee* (careful with *ee* — can get thin)
- Avoid consonant traffic jams on long notes (*strengths*, *worlds*)
- 1–5 words ideal for pop; longer titles need rhythmic identity (*"I Don't Love You Like I Used To"*)
- Stress pattern must match the melodic landing (see prosody.md)

---

## 6. Variation without losing identity

Final chorus tricks:
- last-line twist (*"don't call me up… unless you're coming home"*)
- add a harmony word
- drop instruments under the title once, then full hit

Don't rewrite the title into a synonym each chorus (*forever / always / eternally*) — that's AI drift.

---

## 7. Checklist

- [ ] Title speakable and searchable?
- [ ] Hook identifiable on nonsense syllables?
- [ ] Placement on a stable, stressed landing?
- [ ] Verses make the title heavier?
- [ ] No cliché pair forced only to rhyme the title?
