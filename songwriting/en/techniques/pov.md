# Point of view — distance, address, and who owns the camera

POV chooses what the listener is allowed to know and how close they stand to the heat.

---

## 1. Basic persons

| POV | Form | Strength | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **First (I/we)** | confessional, diary, anthem | intimacy, ownership | self-indulgence, vague "I feel" |
| **Second (you)** | direct address | punch, accusation, seduction | unclear who "you" is |
| **Third (he/she/they)** | camera / story | range, character songs | coldness, ballad-distance |

**We** can be lovers, gang, generation, or false unity — decide which.

---

## 2. Direct address modes

- **To a lover** — default pop; specify enough that it isn't Everylover
- **To the self** — mirror talk; works in bridge
- **To a third party overheard** — dramatic irony
- **To the listener** — fourth wall; use rarely, hits hard

If "you" shifts target mid-song without a signal, clarity dies.

---

## 3. Narrative distance

| Distance | Traits |
|---|---|
| **Close** | body detail, present tense, unfinished thoughts |
| **Middle** | scene + some summary |
| **Far** | reportage, past perfect, wide morals |

Move distance between sections for contrast:
- Verse close-camera  
- Chorus slightly wider claim  
- Bridge closest (whisper) or farthest (verdict)

---

## 4. Unreliable and partial knowers

Powerful when the singer doesn't understand their own story yet.
- Show contradiction between claim and detail (*"I'm over it"* + keeping the sweater)
- Don't explain the irony; let the image do it

---

## 5. POV shifts (allowed if marked)

Legal shifts:
- V1 "I" story → chorus "we" anthem (join the room)
- Story in third → bridge in second (turn to face them)
- Past-tense verse → present chorus (memory → wound now)

Illegal-feeling shifts:
- random person changes line to line
- "you" that sometimes means self, sometimes ex, unlabeled

---

## 6. Character songs

Third-person or first-as-mask:
- Give the character a **want**, a **tell** (repeated gesture), a **lexicon**
- Avoid biography dump; one job, one night, one object often enough

---

## 7. Checklist

- [ ] Person chosen on purpose?
- [ ] "You" has a stable target?
- [ ] Distance changes with section job?
- [ ] Irony shown, not footnoted?
- [ ] Any POV shift has a structural reason?
