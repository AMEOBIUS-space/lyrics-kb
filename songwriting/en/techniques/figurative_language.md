# Figurative language — compression without fog

Figures of speech earn their keep when they **compress** experience. They fail when they decorate emptiness.

---

## 1. Workhorse figures

| Figure | Job | Watch-out |
|---|---|---|
| **Metaphor** | A is B | mixed metaphors; dead metaphors (*broken heart* alone) |
| **Simile** | A is like B | "like" stacks; school-essay rhythm |
| **Metonymy** | related stand-in (*the crown* = power) | obscurity |
| **Synecdoche** | part for whole (*hands* = workers) | accidental vagueness |
| **Personification** | nonhuman acts | cartoon if overused |
| **Hyperbole** | scale for feeling | teen diary tone |
| **Understatement** | pressure by quiet | can read as flat |
| **Oxymoron** | clash in two words | gimmick if unmotivated |

---

## 2. One engine per section

Pick a **metaphor family** and stay inside it long enough to pay off:
- weather system
- vehicle / road
- house / rooms
- machinery / signal
- animals
- religion / ritual (handle without cheap blasphemy-as-sauce)

Switching families every line = music-video channel surf = AI smell.

---

## 3. Concrete vehicle, abstract tenor

Tenor = meaning (loneliness). Vehicle = picture ("vending machine light on an empty lot").

Order of preference:
1. vehicle only (listener infers tenor)
2. vehicle + light touch of tenor
3. tenor explained — usually kill this

---

## 4. Anti-slop for figures (EN)

Red-flag metaphors (reinvent or delete):
- heart as glass / stone / cage (alone, unextended)
- drowning in feelings
- fire of desire
- labyrinth of lies
- dancing in the dark (as pure cliché, not placed scene)
- wings / flying free
- demons / angels without a local detail

**Repair move:** keep the emotion; replace the vehicle with something from object writing.

---

## 5. Extended metaphor test

Can you write three lines that develop the same vehicle without naming the emotion?
If yes — strong enough for a verse. If no — image is a sticker, not an engine.

---

## 6. Checklist

- [ ] Figures compress rather than fluff?
- [ ] One family per stretch of lyric?
- [ ] Vehicle is filmable?
- [ ] No mixed metaphor accidents?
- [ ] Cliché vehicles twisted or replaced?
